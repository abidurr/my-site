About Me

I was introduced to coding with HTML at the age of twelve. My indulgence with programming has persisted ever since. My curiosity led me through programming languages including Basic, C++, and then eventually to my favorite of them all, Python. Around the same time I got my hands on an Arduino Uno and discovered the excitement of being able to control hardware. After high school, I ended up becoming a teaching assistant and tutoring students. I taught high school seniors Math and Physics. Subsequently, I ended up going to college in Groningen, the Netherlands, initially pursuing a degree in Physics and Astronomy. Naturally, programming stuck around in my courses and I could not resist picking up courses on Data Science, Databases, Information Security, Machine Learning, and introductory CS courses. I ended up completing a degree in Computational Science; implementing my computer science skills to the world of data in the field of Astronomy and Physics. My path led me through a lot of advanced mathematics and familiarized me to computational languages such as R, Mathematica, MatLab, SQL, and of course, Python again. I also minored in Humanities and had Ethics in STEM courses. After completing my BSc and moving to NYC, I wondered what I wanted to do next. Whatever it would be, it had to do with computers. I completed Cybersecurity courses on EdX. But then I decided to go back to web development; to the HTML et al that sparked my love for coding in the first place. That decision has brought me here.
